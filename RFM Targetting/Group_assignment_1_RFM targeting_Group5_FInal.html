Summary¶

Below exercise consists of analysis of data provided by multichannel gift company. Datasets consist of information about customers, sales campaign, orders, among other information. Our analysis started with loading and cleaning data from CSV files to PostgresSQL DB. We then analysed the datasets further to see how different types of sales and marketing evolve over time and how different marketing methods impact associated order placement/sales in Christmas and non-Christmas periods. Further analysis involves channel summary of Recency / Frequency / Monetary (RFM) information analysis to find out how the company can target customers properly and effectively. Outcome of the analysis will be used by the company to decide their way forward on marketing campaign in order to maximize gain with better ROI. Analysis was further expanded by adding new dimension to our RFM, known as "interaction" hence, RFMI. Interaction was defined as dimension which explains whether customer responded to campaigns within 7 days after getting catalog and amount of orders placed in that 7 days. This will help the company in designing better strategy for customer relationship management.

Introduction¶

Multichannel gift company has provided their sales campaign, sales and customers data for us to perform an in-depth analysis to derive meaningful insights. The insights will highlight in which areas the company is doing well and where it is lacking. Analysis can help in making better decision leading to a better customer relationship management. Among the aims of the assignment are to concentrate on which campaign strategies are working more effectively, which customer locations are giving better returns, ROI calculations for the campaigns, concentrating on RFM dimensions and how to increase sales during non-Christmas months of year. The information derived from the analysis will be utilized to help the company to produce profitable business decisions in future with better ROI on marketing campaigns.

Below we have summarised the list of datasets we received:

Customer File: It contains information about 100,051 customers. It mainly contains information about customers such as customers' buying activity during particular seasons and catalog/email counts delivered to customer. It also has overlay demographic and psycographic information about customers collected from vendor and merged into it.

Contact File: It has 3,389,329 marketing contact data from 2005-2007. It contains information about which campaign channel was used to contact client and when.

Order File: It has 241,366 records about orders placed by customers online or visiting stores from 2001-2007.

Line Item File: It has 618,661 line item records which can be merged with Order File based on customer id to add information about price, gift or not data to order file.

All above datasets are provided as simple CSV files which will need to be loaded into PostgreSQL DB after doing cleaning and proper data handling.

Our analysis has tried to answer the list of below questions:

  • How do different types of sales and marketing evolve over time?
  • How do different marketing methods impact associated order placement/sales in Christmas and non-Christmas periods?
  • How RFM dimensions impacting performance of particular segment?
  • What changes to be made in order to gain greater ROI for money invested in campaigns?
  • What other dimensions can impact customers' reaction along with RFM?
  • How to use that other dimensions to influence customers hence, improving ROI for campaigns?

2A. 1 Time series trend and seasonality analysis: how do different types of sales and marketing evolve over time?¶

1(a) Trend analysis: how do sales from different order method change over time?¶

Here we conduct the historical time series analysis of different order methods' total sales amount to study its correlation with time. Our key findings are in below:

  • Phone has historically been the dominant order method by sales amount
  • Internet order has been increasing over the year and has exceeded sales by phone for the first time in 2007
  • Store purchasing has also been increasing over the year, and is less affected by seasonality compared to other order methods
  • Despite different levels of impact, all four order methods have a hike around Christmas time, indicating more sales volume in Christmas time. This is more significant for phone and internet orders and less significant for mail and shopping mall orders

2A. 2 How do different marketing methods impact associated order placement/sales in Christmas and non-Christmas periods?¶

Here we split marketing contacts of the two groups, catalog and email, into different periods, Christmas and non-Christmas seasons. We calculate the orders, number of items purchased as well as spending amount associated with the marketing efforts.

Definitions/assumptions used:

  • Associated orders: for catalog shopping, we consider phone and mail orders that are within 7 calendar days of contact dates; for email shopping, we consider internet orders that are within 7 calendar days of contact dates. For store orders/sales, it is difficult to relate it to one specific marketing effort so we do not consider it in this analysis.

  • Response rate: total number of associated orders/total number of contacts

  • Christmas season: contact date is in between Nov 01 to Dec 24 in 2005-2007.

  • NChristmas season: contact date is in between Jan 01 to Oct 31 and post Dec 24 in 2005-2007.

Our key findings are in below:

  • For both email and catalog marketing, average number of orders, average number of items purchased and average spending are higher in Christmas season vs Non-Christmas season.
  • For catalog marketing, average number of orders, average number of items purchased and average spending is higher than email marketing in both Christmas season vs Non-Christmas season.
  • This is mainly due to higher response rate of catalog marketing compared to email marketing. However, from a per response perspective, average spending per response and average items purchased tend to be higher for catalog shopping compared to email shopping, whereas the average number of order placed per response tend to be higher for email marketing. This seem to suggest although email marketing tend to lead to slightly higher number of orders being placed, the items purchased associated with email marketing and the average spendings are less.

CREATE VIEW Christmas_C_spend_items AS
(WITH Christmas_C_spend_items AS
(SELECT Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id, contactdate, contacttype,
SUM(CASE WHEN catalog_shopping.orderdate>contactdate AND catalog_shopping.orderdate<contactdate+7 
    THEN linedollars ELSE 0 END) OVER (PARTITION BY contactdate,Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id ) AS total_spend_catalog,
COUNT(CASE WHEN catalog_shopping.orderdate>contactdate AND catalog_shopping.orderdate<contactdate+7 
      THEN catalog_shopping.ordernum ELSE NULL END) OVER (PARTITION BY contactdate,Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id ) AS total_items_catalog
FROM 
(Christmas_C_contacts LEFT JOIN catalog_shopping
ON Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id=catalog_shopping.cust_id)
)
SELECT cust_id, contactdate, contacttype, AVG(total_spend_catalog) AS total_spend_catalog, AVG(total_items_catalog) AS total_items_catalog
FROM Christmas_C_spend_items
GROUP BY cust_id, contactdate, contacttype)


CREATE VIEW Christmas_C_orders AS
(WITH Christmas_C_orders AS
(SELECT Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id, contactdate, contacttype,
COUNT(CASE WHEN orders_catalog.orderdate>contactdate AND orders_catalog.orderdate<contactdate+7 
      THEN orders_catalog.ordernum ELSE NULL END) OVER (PARTITION BY contactdate,Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id ) AS total_orders_catalog
FROM 
(Christmas_C_contacts LEFT JOIN orders_catalog
ON Christmas_C_contacts.cust_id=orders_catalog.cust_id)
)
SELECT cust_id, contactdate, contacttype, AVG(total_orders_catalog) AS total_orders_catalog

CREATE VIEW catalog_effect_Christmas AS

Question 2B: Impact of RFM dimensions on various segements to gain information on best performing campaign type:¶

Objective : We'll be identifying segments using the RFM dimensions (5 quantiles for each dimension) and estimate response rates for each RFM cell based on the training set. We then use it to make a decision on how many to mail. We'll further validate analysis by using test set to check performance of analysis. Validation will be done by calculating the ROI.

Below analysis consist of 4 major steps:

  • Deriving RFM table with segments data.
  • Splitting data into 30% for doing analysis for making decision and 70% for validating decision made on first 30% data.
  • Combining orders data with contacts data to perform analysis on train data(30%).
  • Validation on test data (70%).

Step 1. RFM table¶

CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW order_dollar AS(
 SELECT line_cleaned."Cust_ID",
    line_cleaned."OrderNum",
    line_cleaned."OrderDate",
    sum(line_cleaned."LineDollars") AS order_spend
   FROM line_cleaned
  GROUP BY line_cleaned."Cust_ID", line_cleaned."OrderNum", line_cleaned."OrderDate");

CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW rfm AS(
SELECT "Cust_ID", CAST(recency AS text) || CAST(frequency AS text) || CAST(monetary AS text) AS rfm_cell 
FROM(
    SELECT order_dollar."Cust_ID", 
    MAX(order_dollar."OrderDate") AS last_purchase, 
    COUNT(order_dollar."OrderNum") AS total_purchase_number, 
    AVG(order_dollar.order_spend) AS average_purchase_amount,
    NTILE(5)OVER(ORDER BY MAX(order_dollar."OrderDate") ASC) recency,
    NTILE(5)OVER(ORDER BY COUNT(order_dollar."OrderNum") ASC) frequency,
    NTILE(5)OVER(ORDER BY AVG(order_dollar.order_spend) ASC) monetary
    FROM order_dollar
    GROUP by order_dollar."Cust_ID") AS rfm_tmp
ORDER BY rfm_cell)

Step 3. Combine orders with contacts to gain overall sight of training dataset¶

We define 'response' as 'once a contact has a related order within 7 days'.

  • seg_cust_num: number of customer in each segment
  • cont_cust: number of customer ever contacted
  • contact_rate: cont_cust/seg_cust_num
  • res_cust: number of customer ever responded (= one of his contact was responded)
  • seg_total_responded_contact: number of contact ever responded
  • seg_total_contact: total number of contact in the segment
  • cont_response_rate: seg_total_responded_contact/seg_total_contact [USE]
  • seg_total_cost: 1 per catalog * total contact
  • seg_total_profit: 30 per order * total order
  • seg_roi: seg_total_profit/seg_total_cost
  • seg_total_gain: seg_total_profit - seg_total_cost

Question 2C: Adding interaction Dimension to RFM to better decision making¶

Not every customer responds to market campaigns in the same way. Some of them may be prone to marketing, while others may be deterred by frequent advertising. Therefore, we have decided to add another dimension to the RFM segment: interaction.

Interaction is defined by the average number of orders a customer placed within 7 days of being mailed a catalog. For our purpose, given average profit per order is 30 while every cost of mailing is 1, as long as a customer place more than 1 order per 30 mails, we would be at break-even point.

Therefore we segment the interaction (I) dimension as below:

  • If the average number of interaction is above 1/30, I=3 (this group of customers would bring us profit)
  • If the average number of interaction is below 1/30 while above 0, I=2 (this group of customers respond to campaigns, but the profit they bring in doesn't cover the mailing cost)
  • If the average number of interaction = 0, I=1 (this group of customers do not respond to campaigns at all)

Below are list of steps which will be performed as part of this analysis:

  • Deriving RFM table with segments data.
  • Spliting data into 30% for doing analysis for making decision and 70% for validating decision made on first 30% data.
  • Combining Orders data with contacts data to perform analysis on train data(30%).
  • Validation on test data(70%).

CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW interaction_new AS(
SELECT *,
    (CASE 
        WHEN num_interact>0.033 THEN 3
        WHEN num_interact<=0.033 AND num_interact>0 THEN 2
        ELSE 1
    END) AS interact
FROM
(SELECT "Cust_ID", AVG(order_related) AS num_interact
FROM contact_ordernum
GROUP BY "Cust_ID") AS avg_interact
)

CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW rfmi AS(
SELECT rfm."Cust_ID",
(rfm_cell || CAST(interact AS text)) AS rfmi_cell
FROM rfm INNER JOIN interaction_new
ON rfm."Cust_ID" = interaction_new."Cust_ID"
)

Conclusion¶

After performing an in-depth analysis on the dataset, we were able to derive a list of meaningful insights. First, we discovered that phone has been a dominant method for sales whereas internet orders increase day by day and crosses sales by phone for the first time in 2007. We also noticed that store purchasing is least impacted by seasonality. Nevertheless, Christmas shows a highest amount of sales for the company.

We also noticed that catalog marketing has a stable patterns over years whereas e-mail marketing shows no such pattern. It also came to our attention that from a per response perspective, average spending per response and average items purchased tend to be higher for catalog shopping as compared to email shopping, whereas the average number of order placed per response tend to be higher for email marketing.

This seems to suggest although email marketing tend to lead to slightly higher number of orders being placed, the items purchased associated with email marketing and the average spendings are less. About segmenats, we discovered that after first 60, response rate is not able to cover cost of marketing. We were able to discover that company gains most profit when only considered first 21 segments for calculation,s hence customers from these segments should not be bothered with more than one mail per day.

We also noticed that through RFM targeting, response rate increased from 2% to 4 % and ROI reached 1.43. When we considered interaction as our dimension along with RFM, we only considered 79 segments of 268 which increased response rate from 4% of RFM to 7.25% of RFMI and also increases ROI from 1.43 to 2.28, hence generating a higher profit.
